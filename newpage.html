Tableau Visualization of Cognitive Biases


Above is a visualization of many different cognitive biases we can be subject to.
The graph is broken down as follows:

  1. What Should We Remember?(Green)
    • Peak-end rule: We remember peak moments and the ending of an event
    • Suggestibility: We perceive ideas told by others as our own memories
    • Google Effect: Tendency to forget easily found online information
    • Etc.
  2. A Need For Quick Action(Yellow)
    • IKEA Effect: We tend to overvalue goods created by ourselves
    • Zero-risk bias: We try to diminish a risk of a rare event rather than a more
      common risk(fear of plane crashes instead of car crashes)
    • Belief bias: If we like the conclusion, we tend to believe in arguments leading to it more
    • Etc.
  3. Missing Information, Assumptions Needed(Blue)
    • Self-Consistency Bias: We tend to change in our mind order and facts about events,
      emotions, and thoughts
    • Cheerleader Effect: Bias which causes people to think individuals are more attractive
      when in a group
    • Bandwagon Effect: It is easier to accept a belief when others have already adopted it
    • Etc.
  4. Too Much Information, Filtering Needed(Red)
    • Framing Effect: Bias where people deiced on options based on whether the options
      are presented with a positive or negative connotation
    • Confirmation Bias: Tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information
      in a way that affirms one's prior beliefs
    • Bias Blind Spot: Recognizing impact of biases on others, but ignoring the same impacts
      in yourself
    • Etc.
